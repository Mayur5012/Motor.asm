Every Production Lamborghini Ever Made


1. LAMBORGHINI 350 GT (1964–1966)
The Lamborghini 350 GT was a grand tourer manufactured by Lamborghini between 1964 and 1966. It was the first production vehicle produced by Lamborghini. The 350 GT was based on the earlier Lamborghini 350 GTV and was equipped with a 3.5 liter V12 engine and a 2-door coupé body by Carrozzeria Touring.
Engine: 3,464 cc (211.4 cu in) 60° aluminium V12 DOHC
Designer: Carrozzeria Touring
Body style: 2-door coupé





>
2. LAMBORGHINI 400 GT (1966-1968)
The first 400 GT, commonly referred to as simply the 400 GT or 400 GT Interim, was essentially the older 350 GT featuring an enlarged, 3,929 cc V12 engine, with a power output of 320 bhp (239 kW). Twenty-three of these cars were built, with three featuring aluminium bodywork.
Engine: 3,929 cc V12
Designer: Carrozzeria Touring
Body style: 2-door coupé





3. LAMBORGHINI MIURA (1966–1972)
The Lamborghini Miura is a sports car produced by Italian automaker Lamborghini between 1966 and 1973. The car was the first supercar with a rear mid-engined two-seat layout, although the concept was first seen in a production road car with René Bonnet's Matra Djet, introduced in 1964.
Engine: 3929 cc V12
Designer: Marcello Gandini at Bertone
Body style: 2-door coupé





4. LAMBORGHINI ESPADA (1968–1978)
The Lamborghini Espada is a 4-seat grand touring coupé built by Italian car manufacturer Lamborghini between 1968 and 1978.
Engine: 3.9 L Lamborghini V12
Designer: Marcello Gandini at Bertone
Length: 4,730 mm (186.2 in)





5. LAMBORGHINI ISLERO (1968–1969)
The Lamborghini 350 GT was a grand tourer manufactured by Lamborghini between 1964 and 1966. It was the first production vehicle produced by Lamborghini. The 350 GT was based on the earlier Lamborghini 350 GTV and was equipped with a 3.5 liter V12 engine and a 2-door coupé body by Carrozzeria Touring.
Engine: 3,464 cc (211.4 cu in) 60° aluminium V12 DOHC
Designer: Carrozzeria Touring

6. LAMBORGHINI JARAMA (1970–1976)
The Lamborghini Jarama (Spanish pronunciation: [xaˈɾama]) is a 2+2 grand tourer manufactured and marketed by Italian car manufacturer Lamborghini between 1970 and 1976. It was styled by Bertone designer Marcello Gandini.
Engine: 3.9 L (3,929 cc) Lamborghini V12
Designer: Marcello Gandini at Bertone
Width: 1,820 mm





7. LAMBORGHINI URRACO (1973–1979)
The Lamborghini Urraco is a 2+2 sports car manufactured by Italian automaker Lamborghini, introduced at the Turin Auto Show in 1970, marketed for model years 1972-1979, and named after a line of Miura-bred fighting bulls; the name translates to "little bull".
Designer: Marcello Gandini at Bertone
Body style: 2+2 coupé
Wheelbase: 2,450 mm





8. LAMBORGHINI COUNTACH (1974–1990)
The Lamborghini Countach (/ˈkuːntɑːʃ/) is a rear mid-engine, rear-wheel-drive sports car produced by the Italian automobile manufacturer Lamborghini from 1974 to 1990. ... The style was introduced to the public in 1970 as the Lancia Stratos Zero concept car.
Body style: 2-door coupé
Transmission: 5-speed synchromesh manual
Height: 1,070 mm





9. LAMBORGHINI SILHOUETTE (1976–1979)
The Lamborghini Silhouette P300 is a two-door two-seat mid-engined rear-wheel drive sports car, that was made in small numbers by Lamborghini between 1976 and 1979. Officially presented at the 1976 Geneva Auto Show, the Silhouette began as a styling exercise by noted Italian Carrozzeria Bertone.
Engine: 2,995.8 cc (182.8 cu in) V8
Width: 1,880 mm (74.0 in)
Predecessor: Lamborghini Urraco





10. LAMBORGHINI JALPA (1981–1988)
The Lamborghini Jalpa (Spanish pronunciation: [ˈxalpa]) is an entry level sports car produced by the Italian automotive manufacturer Lamborghini from 1981 to 1988. ... The Jalpa was the last Lamborghini to use a V8 engine until the Urus.
Engine: 3.5-litre Lamborghini V8
Designer: Giulio Alfieri; Carrozzeria Bertone
Predecessor: Lamborghini Silhouette





11. LAMBORGHINI LM002 (1986–1993)
The Lamborghini LM002 aka "Lamborghini truck" is an off-road truck manufactured by Lamborghini between 1986 and 1993. ... With the idea of using a front mounted Countach V12 to power the LM001 came the next model, the "LM002", which was the first of the three to see actual production by Lamborghini.
Engine: 5.2 L (5167 cc) V12; 7.2 L L804 marine V12
Curb weight: 2,700 kg (5,952 lb)
Body style: 4-door pickup; 4-door sport utility truck





12. LAMBORGHINI DIABLO (1990–2001)
The Lamborghini Diablo is a high-performance mid-engine sports car built by Italian automotive manufacturer Lamborghini between 1990 and 2001. It is the first production Lamborghini capable of attaining a top speed in excess of 320 kilometres per hour (200 mph). ... The name Diablo means "devil" in Spanish.
Engine: 5.7 L V12; 6.0 L V12
Transmission: 5-speed manual
Body style: 2-door coupé; 2-door retractable hard-top convertible (roadster)





13. LAMBORGHINI MURCIELAGO (2001–2010)
The Lamborghini Murciélago is a sports car produced by Italian automotive manufacturer Lamborghini between 2001 and 2009. ... Production of the Murciélago ended on 5 November 2010, with a total production run of 4,099 cars. Its successor, the Aventador, was unveiled at the 2011 Geneva Motor Show.
Engine: 6.2 L–6.5 L V12
Transmission: 6-speed manual; 6-speed e-gear automated manual
Designer: Luc Donckerwolke





14. LAMBORGHINI GALLARDO (2003–2013)
The Lamborghini Gallardo (/ɡaɪˈjɑːrdoʊ/; Spanish: [ɡaˈʎaɾðo]) is a sports car built by the Italian automotive manufacturer Lamborghini from 2003 to 2013. It is Lamborghini's best-selling model with 14,022 built throughout its production run. ... The Gallardo was replaced by the Huracán in 2014.
Model years: 2004–2014
Transmission: 6-speed manual; 6-speed e-gear automated manual
Engine: 5.0 L even firing V10 (1st Generation); 5.2 L Lamborghini odd firing V10 (2nd Generation)

16. LAMBORGHINI HURACAN (2014–Present)
Lamborghini Huracan is a 2 seater Coupe available in a price range of ₹ 3.22 - 4.10 Crore. It is available in 3 variants, 1 engine option and 1 transmission option : Automatic (Dual Clutch). 17. LAMBORGHINI URUS (2018–Present)
Lamborghini Urus is the first Super Sport Utility Vehicle in the world to merge the soul of a super sports car with the functionality of an SUV. Powered by a 4.0-liter twin-turbo V8 engine producing 650 CV and 850 Nm of torque, Urus accelerates from 0 to 62 mph in 3.6 seconds and reaches a top speed of 190 mph.
